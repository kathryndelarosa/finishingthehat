I speak in binaries & oxymorons (& ampersands) and think of it like this: these two things are not the edges of possibility, but two points in an expanse without borders. In the tarot, Temperance is a balancing act. It’s that two extremes can coexist, have to coexist, to make a whole. Temperance is neither compromise or concession, but an ebb and flow, settling into alignment. For Temperance to encompass two disparate ideas is to conjunct them, to heal a schism, to honor the past & preserve its good in a revolutionary future.

I really love Temperance. I relate to its contradictions, as a theater maker and political thinker. I’m American and not, I am man & woman and mystical & rational, I have a Pisces sun & a Virgo moon, I conserve & abolish, beyond nationalism, but not without it.

I tend to like linear, conventional drama, with queer people of color and set anywhere but New York. Depending on audience, I think content can be radical independent of form, and we have more to learn about each other than we do about theater.

I write about history & how it haunts us, in elusive, ancestral pain & its materialist conception: how centuries of global capitalism have located these characters in their current conditions, however mundane or insular or provincial their daily lives seem. I like plays where nothing seems to happen. I think there is joy in the banal, life is more than suffering, and the vapid, routine things we do are worth celebration. I believe in specificity, from "get the name of the dog" to the narrative masterclass that is All Too Well by Taylor Swift. I write womxn who swear and scream and hurt and care for each other, who are strong only some of the time, who are too much. I love realism because life contains magic. I'm interested in rurality, ritual, community, epiphany, divinity.

Temperance is a vessel for many things & multiple realities & is, ultimately, dialogic: Temperance holds truth. And I’ve pursued truth from birth, in waxing and waning faith, in journalism’s first obligation is to the truth, in astrology, in writing a thesis on verbatim drama, in archives & memory & myriad ways to keep record. Temperance is Yuri Kochiyama’s instruction of don’t become too narrow.

The theater I strive for is jubilant and full of hope borne from loss, The Star following The Tower. I come from resilience and survival, at the twice-colonized fringes of empire and its very heart. I look at my home in Kentucky and see struggle & strength against oligarchy. I've lived two decades in the middle of America and wrenched it open to fit me and my contradictions. I write with hope because we imagine and create compassionate, anarchic futures to care for ourselves and each other.

But then again, the Uranus tattooed on my arm corresponds to The Fool.

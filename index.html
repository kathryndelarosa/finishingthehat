Kathryn de la Rosa (b. 1997, Prestonsburg, Kentucky) is a Pisces and a dramaturg, playwright and multimedia producer based in Kentucky and southern Indiana. She is currently a dramaturgy & literary management apprentice in the Professional Training Company at Actors Theatre of Louisville.

Kathryn earned a B.A. in theatre & drama and journalism from Indiana University Bloomington, where she worked on mainstage and student productions as a dramaturg, technician, and performer. Her plays have been developed in IU’s At First Sight new play festival and by University Players. She graduated cum laude with a thesis on Mike Daisey’s appearance on This American Life.

Her recent credits include:
God, staged reading of Bonnets (How Ladies of Good Breeding Are Induced to Murder) by Jen Silverman
Production dramaturg, Vinegar Tom by Caryl Churchill
Production dramaturg, Haus of Mirth by Kaela Mei-Shing Garvin
Sound designer, Titus Andronicus by William Shakespeare

Previously, she served as a literary assistant in the 2019 New Harmony Project Conference, and interned in the literary office at Asolo Repertory Theatre. She is a 2013 creative alumna of the Kentucky Center Governor’s School for the Arts, where she returned on summer staff in 2016.
